Guide · Android

The future, on your home screen.

A watchlist app and two home-screen widgets — a TradingView-style board and a single-market chart — that keep Manifold probabilities in the corner of your eye, refreshed on a battery-friendly schedule.

Step 1 · Install

Three taps to running

  1. Download the APK from the latest release on your phone.
  2. Open it and allow the install — Android asks once to permit installs from your browser. Updates install over the top; your watchlist survives.
  3. Open Mantic View and add your first market. Then long-press your home screen → Widgets → find the two ManticView widgets.
Step 2 · The watchlist app

Where you curate the future

  1. Add or search. Paste a market slug or URL and tap Add, or type words ("world cup") and Search — results add with one tap. Multiple-choice markets track their top answer first; switch or add more answers anytime.
  2. Last updated & Refresh all — the time of the newest data, and a button to re-fetch everything in parallel.
  3. Theme — System, Light, or Dark, applied to the app and both widgets.
  4. Group headers carry the controls: ↑ ↓ reorder groups, ✎ renames (widgets follow along), and Hide removes a group from the watchlist widget while it keeps tracking in the app.
  5. Each market row: the Δ chip picks its comparison period (1H · 6H · 1D · 1W · 1M · ALL); the ⋮ menu moves it, switches or adds answers, and changes its group; ✕ removes exactly that entry.

  1. Tap the header to open the app; tap any market to open it on manifold.markets.
  2. Last updated time, always visible.
  3. ↻ refreshes on demand — it becomes a spinner while fetching.
  4. Groups render as sections, in the order you set in the app; hidden groups don't appear here.
  5. The list scrolls when your watchlist outgrows the widget.
  6. The trend line shows the change over that market's own comparison period — "▲ 3 pts · 1d" means up three points in a day.
  7. Resolved markets show their outcome in color for 48 hours, then quietly retire to the app's Resolved section.
Step 4 · The market widget (2×2)

One market, full chart

  1. Placing it asks two questions: which watchlist (group) to follow — or all markets — and which market to start on.
  2. The big number is the live probability; the delta beside it uses the market's comparison period from the app.
  3. The chart is real history — seeded from up to 1,000 actual bets the moment you add a market, so it's never empty.
  4. ‹ › flip through your watchlist, wrapping at the ends; the counter shows where you are. Tap the middle to open the market.

Step 5 · Under the hood

How the data flows

Manifold public API ──▶ seed: last 1,000 bets ──▶ on-device history (288 pts) ▲ │ └── background refresh every 30 min ◀──────────┴──▶ widgets re-render
  1. Battery-friendly by design: background refresh runs every 30 minutes, only when online, and stops entirely when you remove your last widget.
  2. Honest math: period deltas are interpolated at the exact cutoff time, so "1H" compares against one hour ago even between refreshes.
  3. Private by default: everything lives on your device. No account, no API key, no telemetry — just anonymous reads of Manifold's public API.
